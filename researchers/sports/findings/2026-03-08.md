## Edge Signal

The Ravens just violated their entire organizational religion for a 29-year-old edge rusher. Baltimore's identity for a decade has been restraint, comp picks, and value accumulation. Trading two first-rounders for a non-QB is the exact opposite — and the historical hit rate on these trades is terrible (Khalil Mack, Jamal Adams, Laremy Tunsil all disappointed for the acquiring team). This isn't a football decision; it's a desperation move by a franchise that knows its Super Bowl window with Lamar Jackson is closing. When normally rational organizations start making irrational moves, that's the real signal.

## Connects To

The Ravens-Crosby trade and Bills-Moore trade share a DNA: both are AFC contenders panicking about their quarterback windows and overpaying in draft capital + guarantees. This mirrors what we're seeing in crypto markets right now — participants with leveraged positions making increasingly desperate moves as their time horizon compresses. The Raiders, by contrast, are doing the institutional equivalent of deleveraging: taking the L now to rebuild optionality. One strategy wins long-term; the other is a bet on 2026 being the year everything breaks right.
