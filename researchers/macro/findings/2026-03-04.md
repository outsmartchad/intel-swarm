# Macro Economist - 2026-03-04

## Top Findings

- **Dollar's reserve share silently breached 40% floor** — The USD's share of global central bank reserves has fallen below 40% for the first time, while central banks quietly accelerate gold accumulation as a hedge against weaponized dollar sanctions. This is structural, not cyclical. [Source](https://www.theinteldrop.org/2026/02/24/the-dollar-weakens-falling-below-40-of-global-reserves-and-brics-advance-in-alternative-systems/)

- **Fed quietly restarted asset purchases while calling it something else** — The Fed halted QT in late 2025 and launched "reserve management purchases" (buying short-duration bonds to maintain ample reserves). They insist this isn't QE. It is functionally QE. With Kevin Warsh nominated to replace Powell in May, markets are pricing a "QT-for-cuts" framework — cut short rates, simultaneously accelerate bond sales. The contradiction is unresolved. [Source](https://markets.financialcontent.com/wral/article/marketminute-2026-2-27-the-great-2026-twist-us-treasury-yields-diverge-as-geopolitical-unrest-and-fed-transitions-roil-markets)

- **BRICS Pay has already cut USD usage in intra-bloc trade by ~two-thirds** — India officially assumed BRICS presidency and is quietly building the infrastructure while publicly saying "we don't want to replace the dollar." BRICS Pay (links Russia's SPFS, China's CIPS, India's UPI), the BRICS Unit (gold-backed settlement instrument), and CBDC interoperability frameworks are all moving from planning to live deployment. [Source](https://watcher.guru/news/brics-de-dollarization-agenda-for-2026-advances-with-global-launch)

- **IMF survey: nearly half of respondents expect sovereign debt crisis "in coming months"** — Record debt in the US, UK, France, Italy, and Japan is not a distant risk — it's the Davos consensus. Ray Dalio's warnings from January are increasingly corroborated by sovereign bond market behavior. The 10-year Treasury is at 4.09% and falling even as short rates stay pinned — the bond market is pricing slowdown, not inflation. [Source](https://english.elpais.com/economy-and-business/2026-02-08/public-debt-a-ticking-time-bomb-about-to-explode.html)

- **Capital is flowing INTO emerging markets, not out** — The conventional "flight to USD safety" trade is reversing. EM ETFs drew $4B+ in January 2026, EM stocks/bonds/currencies all surging on dollar weakness. Institutional fund managers are systematically underweight EM and are rotating in, suggesting the move has room to run. [Source](https://www.fxleaders.com/news/2026/02/27/global-capital-flows-shift-toward-emerging-markets/)
