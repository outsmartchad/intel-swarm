# Commodities Researcher - 2026-03-07

## Top Findings

- **Strait of Hormuz effectively closed** — US "Operation Epic Fury" strikes on Iran (Feb 28) reportedly killed Supreme Leader Khamenei; Iran's IRGC retaliated by declaring the strait closed to commercial traffic, cutting off ~20% of global seaborne oil (20M bbl/day) and triggering Brent above $80. [Source](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/international/us/global-oil-supply-chains-face-historic-stress-crude-prices-and-tanker-rates-now-at-all-time-highs-heres-the-key-reason-behind-the-oil-price-surge/articleshow/129107376.cms)

- **Tanker rates at all-time record** — Shipping 2M barrels from US Gulf to Asia now costs $29M, freight prices doubled in 2 weeks; transport alone = ~$14.50/bbl, roughly 20% of WTI's current price — a structural cost embedded in everything downstream. [Source](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/international/us/global-oil-supply-chains-face-historic-stress-crude-prices-and-tanker-rates-now-at-all-time-highs-heres-the-key-reason-behind-the-oil-price-surge/articleshow/129107376.cms)

- **Fertilizer +6.5% MoM, right before spring planting** — Hormuz closure stranded ~1/3 of global traded fertilizer; February 2026 saw a 6.5% fertilizer price spike + 2.1% consumer food price rise MoM, hitting just as Northern Hemisphere farmers need inputs for spring planting — a multi-year food cycle shock is now baked in. [Source](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-3-4-geopolitical-firestorm-fertilizer-prices-surge-65-as-operation-epic-fury-paralyzes-global-supply-chains)

- **Silver entering price-discovery territory with institutional inflows** — After a 147% surge in 2025, silver has broken key resistance; Sprott reporting "significant institutional inflows into silver globally," GoldSilver analyst calls $100+ silver in 2026 as supply deficits deepen and industrial demand (solar, AI hardware) accelerates. [Source](https://sprott.com/insights/gold-silver-outlook-2026/)

- **DRC cobalt quota cuts supply 50% through 2027** — The DRC swapped its export ban for a quota regime that slashes cobalt shipments by 50% for 2026–27, causing a price rebound that's largely unreported; battery supply chains dependent on cobalt cathodes are now racing to find alternatives or stockpile. [Source](https://www.mining.com/coppers-tight-supply-and-tariff-risks-set-for-a-volatile-2026/)

## Edge Signal
The Hormuz closure is simultaneously a food crisis trigger — fertilizer stranded at sea means the 2026 spring planting season is already compromised, and the food shock from this won't hit retail shelves until Q3/Q4 2026 at the earliest, meaning the market hasn't priced it yet.

## Connects To
The copper shortage driven by AI data center demand + Hormuz oil shock = a double commodity squeeze on the hardware layer of the AI buildout, directly threatening the physical infrastructure that autonomous agents and on-chain compute depend on.
