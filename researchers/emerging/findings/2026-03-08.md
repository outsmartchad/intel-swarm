# Emerging Markets Researcher - 2026-03-08

## Top Findings

- **Ethiopia 180% YoY stablecoin transfer growth after 30% currency devaluation — fastest-growing retail market in Africa**: Following a 30% devaluation of the Ethiopian birr, retail-sized stablecoin transfers surged 180% YoY in 2025. Ethiopia is now the fastest-growing retail stablecoin market in Africa, demonstrating how currency collapse drives immediate, practical stablecoin adoption at the street level. https://transak.com/blog/africa-fintech-stablecoin-report-2026

- **Only 12% of intra-African transactions processed on continent — 88% routed through US/Europe**: IMF data reveals just 12% of intra-African financial transactions are processed domestically. The rest route through US and European correspondent banks. Africa Export Import Bank president: "It is easier for a bank in an African country to finance trade with a European counterpart than with its neighbors." This infrastructure gap is what stablecoin rails are actually solving. https://transak.com/blog/africa-fintech-stablecoin-report-2026

- **Indonesia reclassifies crypto from commodity to financial instrument under OJK — 25 licensed exchanges, $39B annual volume**: In January 2025, Indonesia shifted crypto oversight to the Financial Services Authority (OJK), reclassifying digital assets from commodities to regulated financial instruments. Result: 25 licensed exchanges, Rp86.25T in regulated derivatives trading, Rp1.76T in tax revenue. Indonesia now ranks 4th in Asia-Pacific for on-chain transaction value with 19M+ active users. https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/stocks/indonesia-emerges-as-one-of-the-world-s-top-crypto-markets-report-says-1035718654

- **Kenya mobile money daily usage jumps from 23.6% to 52.6% in 4 years — 93% crypto awareness, 5th globally for stablecoin transactions**: Kenya's daily mobile money usage more than doubled from 23.6% (2021) to 52.6% (2025), while the country now ranks 5th globally for transactional stablecoin use. 93% of Indonesians are crypto-aware, but 7% remain outside the market despite awareness — the largest latent crypto audience globally, waiting on education not incentives. https://www.thunes.com/insights/trends/why-mobile-wallets-are-skyrocketing-in-emerging-markets/

## Edge Signal
The real story in Ethiopia's 180% stablecoin surge isn't adoption — it's that stablecoins have become the automatic response to currency collapse in emerging markets, and nobody is tracking how quickly this pattern will replicate across the 50% of Africa that remains unbanked.
